### Post 1 (Educate)

**Title:** Securing the Foundation: Why Critical Infrastructure Security Matters More Than Ever

**Body:**

From power grids and water treatment plants to transportation systems and financial networks, critical infrastructure is the backbone of our society. But as these systems become more connected, they also become more vulnerable to cyberattacks.

Critical Infrastructure Security is no longer just about physical security; it's about securing the entire digital estate. This means integrating cloud-native security controls (CNAPP) with the central governance (PPPM) and intelligence (SAIL) layers of a Cybersecurity Mesh Architecture (CSMA) 3.0.

It's time to shift our focus from perimeter defense to a more holistic, resilience-based approach to securing our most critical assets.

### Post 2 (Educate)

**Title:** The Convergence of IT and OT: A Security Challenge and Opportunity

**Body:**

The lines between Information Technology (IT) and Operational Technology (OT) are blurring. While this convergence is driving new levels of efficiency and innovation, it's also creating new security risks.

Traditional IT security solutions are often not a good fit for OT environments, which have unique requirements for safety, reliability, and availability. A successful IT/OT security strategy requires a deep understanding of both domains, as well as a commitment to collaboration and shared ownership.

By breaking down the silos between IT and OT, we can build a more secure and resilient future for our critical infrastructure.

### Post 4 (Educate)

**Title:** Building Resilience in Critical Infrastructure

**Body:**

In the world of critical infrastructure, it's not a matter of *if* an incident will occur, but *when*. That's why resilience is so important.

Resilience is the ability to withstand and recover from a cyberattack. It's about more than just preventing attacks; it's about having the people, processes, and technology in place to minimize the impact of an incident and restore operations as quickly as possible.

A resilience-based approach to critical infrastructure security is essential for ensuring the continued availability and reliability of our most essential services.
