> ## 3. Key Insights from Research
> 
> - **The Bottleneck Problem:** Traditional IAM often creates a central bottleneck, slowing down development and business processes. 75% of organizations prioritize business efficiency over robust security, highlighting the need for a more agile approach.
> - **Centralized Policy:** The "Ce" part of CeDeSec aligns with the PPPM layer of CSMA. A central team defines the high-level access policies (e.g., "developers cannot approve their own code to production").
> - **Decentralized Enforcement:** The "DeSec" part involves embedding policy enforcement points directly into the development pipeline and infrastructure (e.g., CI/CD pipeline checks, service mesh policies). This allows for automated, real-time enforcement without manual intervention.
> - **The Role of the Identity Fabric:** The CeDeSec pattern relies on a robust Identity Fabric to provide the consistent identity data needed for the decentralized enforcement points to make decisions.
