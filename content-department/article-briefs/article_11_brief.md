## 2. Details to Include

- **Core Concept:** Address the common fear of automation causing disruption. Introduce the concept of starting with low-risk, high-value tasks like alert enrichment.
- **Closing Sentence:** "The goal of your first automation project should not be to build a fully autonomous SOC, but to give your team back their most valuable resource: time."

## 3. Key Insights from Research

- **The Analyst Burnout Problem:** 71% of SOC staff report high stress, and 55% are considering quitting. Automation is a key strategy for reducing this burnout by handling repetitive tasks.
- **The ROI of Automation:** Automating tasks like phishing email response can reduce the time from 90 minutes to 40 seconds, saving an organization ~70 hours per day and ~$900k per year.
- **Low-Hanging Fruit:** Alert enrichment is a perfect starting point. An automation playbook can automatically gather context for an alert (e.g., user information, device posture, threat intelligence) and present it to the analyst, saving them significant time.
- **The Gains Analysis Framework:** Introduce this framework as a way to identify and prioritize automation opportunities. Start with tasks that have a high "pain" score (time-consuming, error-prone) and a low implementation risk.

## 4. Target Audience

- **Primary:** SOC Managers, Security Operations leaders, and security analysts.
- **Secondary:** CISOs and IT leaders who are hesitant to invest in automation.
