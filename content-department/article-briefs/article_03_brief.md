> ## 3. Key Insights from Research
> 
> - **The Problem with "Big Bang" Adoption:** Many large-scale security projects fail because they are too disruptive and complex. The Strangler Fig Pattern, originally from software development, offers a proven alternative for gradual modernization.
> - **A Practical First Step:** A good starting point is to "strangle" a legacy SIEM by feeding its data into a modern SAIL (Security Analytics & Intelligence Layer). This allows the organization to gain advanced analytics capabilities without immediately decommissioning the old system.
> - **Measuring Success:** Focus on metrics that demonstrate value quickly, such as a reduction in alert triage time or faster detection of specific threat types. This builds the business case for further investment.
> - **Cultural Shift:** CSMA adoption is as much a cultural shift as a technical one. It requires breaking down silos between IT and security teams, which is a challenge in 52% of organizations.
