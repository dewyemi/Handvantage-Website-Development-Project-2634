> ## 3. Key Insights from Research
> 
> - **The Problem of Technical Debt:** Many organizations are burdened by aging security systems (74% of ICS systems are 6-10 years old). These systems are often difficult to integrate and create significant technical debt.
> - **How the Pattern Works:** The Strangler Fig Pattern involves building a new system around the edges of the old one, gradually intercepting traffic and functionality until the old system can be safely decommissioned. This avoids the risk and disruption of a "big bang" migration.
> - **The SIEM Example:** A practical application is to keep the legacy SIEM for log collection but route its data to a new, cloud-native SAIL for advanced analytics, machine learning, and correlation. This provides immediate value without requiring a full SIEM replacement.
> - **Benefits:** This approach reduces risk, demonstrates value quickly, and allows for a more agile and iterative approach to security modernization.
